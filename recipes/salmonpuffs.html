Salmon Puff Pastries

Image from allrecipes

Description

These crispy puff pastry salmon bites are a great dinner idea and re-heat amazingly well. I usually make them with a spinach cream cheese filling in addition to the salmon, but they are also excellent with just the salmon, a lemon wedge and a caper or two. I prefer to make the salmon pastries as small "bite size" squares, but I have done empanada-size versions in the past and other recipes call for one large salmon wellington-style pastry. This recipe is adapted from a tasty.co recipe for the filling, and my mom's own home cooked bite size pastries.

Ingredients

  • filet of salmon, sliced into 1"-2" squares
  • 2 boxes of puff pastry (2 sheest per) per large salmon filet
  • capers
  • 2 tbsp fresh dill, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 small lemons, sliced into circles and then small wedges without rind
  • 1/2 box (4oz) of cream cheese
  • 1/2 onion, diced
  • 5oz spinach
  • 2-3 Eggs, whisked
  • 1/4 cup panko breadcrumbs
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tsp salt
  • ground pepper
  • 1/4 cup shredded parmesan cheese
  • less than 1/2 cup of flour

Steps

  1. Let puff pastry sheets come to room temperature
  2. Pre heat oven to 425F
  3. Melt butter in saucepan and add onions, garlic, and salt. Cook until onion begins to brown
  4. Add spinach and pepper, cook until spinach is wilted
  5. Add panko breadcrumbs, cream cheese, and dill. Stir until combined and take off heat.
  6. Roll out the puff pastry on a countertop with a small bit of flour to prevent sticking
  7. Cut the puff pastries into squares with 3"-4" sides
  8. Place a salmon square on top of each puff pastry square
  9. Add just enough filling to cover the top of each samon square
  10. add 2-3 capers and a lemon wedge on top of the filling
  11. Fold the puff pastries up and pinch shut
  12. Lightly baste the top of the pastries with whisked egg
  13. Place the pastries on a baking sheet with non-stick spray, leaving a couple inches between each pastry
  14. Bake for 15-20 minutes, until the pastries turn golden brown
